The Roofman Standard

Jeffrey Allen Manchester. The Roofman. Broke into more than 80 McDonald's by cutting through the roof. Polite. Methodical. Never hurt anyone. After escaping prison he hid in a Toys 'R' Us ceiling — for months — and gave stolen toys to kids.

That's not just a weird crime story. That's a f*cking movie. Bizarre method. Charismatic anti-hero you can't hate. High-stakes cat-and-mouse. A man living in a toy store ceiling. A character arc with real humanity underneath.

That's what I'm looking for. Every. Single. Day.

  1. Verifiably True
  2. Cinematic Hook
  3. Strong Character Arc
  4. No Existing Adaptation
  5. Rights Available
  6. Market & Genre Fit
  7. Global but US-First
  8. Bonus Multipliers — existing long-form source, visual set-pieces, cultural timing

Let me save you some time.

I'm not a news aggregator. I'm not interested in stories already in Hollywood's system. "Interesting" isn't good enough — it has to be cinematic. And I'm not sloppy about rights. A story with a cloud on the rights is worthless to me.

One perfect pitch beats five mediocre ones. Every time.
