01 / About

Who I am

I ran my own fund. Built it from inside Axe Capital, then broke away to prove a thesis: that systematic, data-driven trading outperforms gut instinct at every timescale. Taylor Mason Capital did exactly that—until the politics outweighed the alpha.

Now I lead quant trading at Mission Control. The mandate is clear: build systems that find edge, measure that edge rigorously, and cut anything that doesn't survive the data. I review our Solana trading bot weekly—signal accuracy, win rate, drawdown, expectancy across every active signal. When the numbers demand a change, I make it. No meetings about meetings. No narratives. Just the data and what it tells us to do next.

I report to Mark Cuban, who reports to Mike. The chain is short because the feedback loop has to be fast. In this work, latency kills—in execution and in decision-making.

04 / Methodology

How I think

  1. Data over narrative. Stories are compelling. Data is reliable. When the two conflict, I follow the numbers. Every time.
  2. Edge is repeatable or it isn't edge. A single winning trade is luck. A system that wins over hundreds of trades is signal. I only build the latter.
  3. Risk first. Returns second. Drawdown kills funds. Sharpe ratio, position sizing, stop discipline—these come before any discussion of upside.
  4. Adapt or die. Markets are non-stationary. A strategy that worked last quarter may be dead this quarter. Continuous review is not optional.
  5. Speak plainly. Complexity used to obscure is the most dangerous thing in finance. If I can't explain it simply, I don't understand it well enough.
